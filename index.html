ABOUT

Oduntan Grace Ayomide is a passionate Theatre Arts student at the University of Lagos, specializing in costume design, stage performance, props management, and creative collaboration. With a strong dedication to visual storytelling, she uses costumes not just as clothing but as powerful tools to define character, culture, and narrative. Grace’s journey in theatre has been marked by diverse and impactful productions. She has worked as an Assistant Costumier in the Department of Theatre Arts (UNILAG) since 2022 and has contributed to several notable plays, including Midnight Hotel and Langbodo by Femi Osofisan, and Moremi by Lekan Balogun. Her role as a Props Manager in Son of the Harem (2025) further showcases her versatility in stagecraft and production design.

As a costume designer, Grace has brought her creativity to life in productions such as Conjugation of Bastard by Idris Munnis at the Lagos Fringe Festival (2023), the Costume & Make-Up Parade (2024), and the 20th UNILAG Carnival (Team Bahamas, 2025). She has also contributed to international-themed projects, including the Festival of Plays (Team South Africa, 2022). Grace approaches every design with curiosity and care, combining research, attention to detail, and imagination to create costumes that do more than dress a character—they help tell the story. She enjoys collaborating with directors, actors, and fellow creatives, believing that the best results come from working as a team to bring a shared vision to the stage. Outside the theatre, Grace finds inspiration in fashion, culture, and history, weaving these influences into her work. For her, costume design is a way to bridge tradition and modernity, creating pieces that are not only visually striking but also deeply meaningful. Her dream is to keep shaping stories through costume and leave lasting impressions both on stage and beyond.
